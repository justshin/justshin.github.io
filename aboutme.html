Hi, my name is Justin Shin! I'm a current 2nd year at UC
Berkeley double majoring in Data Science and Economics.
Ever since I was young, I always knew that my life was
very different from other's around me; having grown up with
a military family, I lived in roughly 12 states and
3 different countries throughout my life and have been
exposed to many different cultures and valuable connections.
Moving almost every year was one of the main reasons why I
appreciate traveling and photography(check out
photography to see more!).

Besides academics in both data science and economics,
I have strong interests in web design and mobile app
development. This website was made extensively with my
knowledge in html,css, and javascript. (To look at other
projects I have worked on, check out the resume/project
tab to see more). Currently, I am working as a student
advisor at the main UC Berkeley Office of Undergraduate
Admissions assisting incoming students/parents one-on-one
and specializing in customer service. I am also working as
a freelance web developer/designer and in the process
of making a brand new social media platform (more coming soon).
In the future, some of my other dreams are to open up a high
end restaurant somewhere in the seattle area due to my love
of cooking/food as well as working in the tech sports industry.

Most of my volunteer/community work focus on the
environment and children. Since high school, I have
worked on countless projects regarding serving
communities as well as working as a volunteer for
children programs. Currently, I have been working
with the Berkeley Project, which is a non-profit
organization at UC Berkeley that serves the Berkeley
community every semester. I have worked as a site leader
(leading over several dozen volunteers) for the program
since coming in as a freshman and plan on continuing
this tradition throughout my undergraduate year.

I am an active member of the Data Science Society at
UC Berkeley. Through this club, I am exposed to
various projects as well as a network of connections
of people who are also interested in working with
data science. Most of my work in data science
involve sport analytics, especially pertaining to
basketball analysis. I also play for the Men's Korean
basketball team at UC Berkeley, which won first place
at the UC tournament this past spring.
